6. Appeals

If you disagree with your Personal Independence Payment (PIP) decision:

  1. Discuss the decision with the Personal Independence Payment team.

    Explain why you do not agree - include if you think something has been overlooked or if your circumstances have changed. The contact details will be on your decision letter.

  2. Make a formal request to have the decision looked at again (known as ‘mandatory reconsideration’) if you still disagree.

    Explain why you’re asking for a reconsideration and include more information if necessary. The contact details will be on your decision letter. You must make the request within 1 month of your decision.

  3. Fill in a ‘Notice of appeal against a decision of the Department of Work and Pensions’ if you disagree with the mandatory reconsideration.

    You must send this within 1 month of your mandatory reconsideration decision.
