2. Eligibility

To get Personal Independence Payment, you must:

  • be aged 16 to 64
  • have a disability or condition that affects you more than half the time
  • have been a resident of England, Scotland or Wales for at least 2 of the last 3 years
  • normally live in England, Scotland or Wales, or live abroad as a member of the armed forces
  • not be subject to immigration control (unless you’re a sponsored immigrant)

You can apply for PIP if you’re living in another European Economic Area (EEA) country or Switzerland.

Your condition does not have to be permanent, but you must have have been affected by it for the last 3 months, and expect it to last at least the next 9 months.

You’ll be assessed on whether your condition affects you:

  • managing your medicines, treatments or therapies
  • seeing, speaking or hearing
  • using the toilet
  • washing or bathing
  • dressing yourself
  • preparing or cooking a meal from fresh ingredients
  • eating or drinking
  • getting out and about
  • mixing with people
  • planning or following a route
  • understanding written information
  • dealing with money
